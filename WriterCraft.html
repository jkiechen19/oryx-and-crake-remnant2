Writer’s Craft

Choose a passage with a strong example of writer’s craft. Name the literary device being used and say how it adds to the effects of the story.

Posted by Jackie on January 31, 2023

Quote:

"He needed to forget the past – the distant past, the immediate past, the past in any form. He needed to exist only in the present, without guilt, without expectation. As the Crakers did. Perhaps a different name would do that for him." (348-349)

In the quote, the author used the word "past" very often at the end of a phrase. The author uses the repetition of the word "past" to show how Snowman is so desperate of the idea of moving on and forget about the past and how desperate Snowman is to change his name. This effects the story in a way because it shows the development of Jimmy's Character to Snowman whose life is very lonely and sad.
